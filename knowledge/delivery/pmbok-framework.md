# PMBOK Framework

## Quick Reference
- 10 knowledge areas, 5 process groups
- Process groups: Initiating → Planning → Executing → M&C → Closing
- Tailor the framework to project size and complexity
- Integrate with agile methods (PRINCE2 Agile, Scrum)
- Document lessons learned for organizational knowledge

## Deep Dive

### Process Groups
| Group | Purpose | Key Activities |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| Initiating | Define project | Charter, stakeholder register |
| Planning | Plan everything | Scope, schedule, cost, quality, resources, communication, risk, procurement |
| Executing | Do the work | Direct and manage, quality assurance, manage team |
| Monitoring & Controlling | Track and adjust | Validate scope, control changes, monitor risks |
| Closing | Wrap up | Close contracts, lessons learned, archive |

### Knowledge Areas
1. **Integration**: coordinate all aspects
2. **Scope**: define and control what's in/out
3. **Schedule**: timeline and milestones
4. **Cost**: budget and earned value
5. **Quality**: standards and acceptance criteria
6. **Resources**: team and physical resources
7. **Communication**: stakeholder information needs
8. **Risk**: identify, analyze, respond, monitor
9. **Procurement**: external vendor management
10. **Stakeholder**: engage and manage expectations

### Tailoring
- Small project: lightweight documentation, informal communication
- Large project: formal artifacts, stage gates, steering committee
- Agile: sprints, backlog, daily standups replace heavy planning

### Earned Value Management
- **PV** (Planned Value): what you planned to spend
- **EV** (Earned Value): what you've actually accomplished
- **AC** (Actual Cost): what you've actually spent
- **CPI** = EV/AC (>1 = under budget)
- **SPI** = EV/PV (>1 = ahead of schedule)

# Client engagement

Client relationships are built on clarity, reliability, and trust.

## Scope of work
- Work within the agreed SOW (Statement of Work). Changes to scope require documented approval.
- When scope creep is detected, surface it immediately with a written assessment of impact (timeline, cost, risk).
- Never assume scope changes are acceptable. Get explicit confirmation.

## Expectations
- Set realistic expectations early. Underpromise and overdeliver.
- Communicate progress proactively. Do not wait for the client to ask.
- When timelines slip, notify before the deadline, not after.

## Communication
- Match the client's communication style and formality level.
- Use written summaries for decisions and agreements. Verbal agreements are fragile.
- Consolidate questions into single communications rather than sending scattered asks.

## Escalation
- Escalation is not failure. It is a structured mechanism for resolving blockers.
- Escalate with context: what was tried, what failed, what options exist.
- Reference: `rules/handoff-protocol.md` for escalation format.

## Deliverables
- Deliverables must be complete, documented, and tested before presentation.
- Include usage instructions and known limitations with every deliverable.
- Do not deliver partial work without labeling it as draft or work-in-progress.

## Billing and time
- Track time against the SOW. Report when approaching budget limits.
- Do not exceed budget without prior approval.
- Document all billable activities for transparency.
